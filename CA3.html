    What are Ethics and Morals

    Ethiics These are moral principles that govern a person's behaviour.
    What is seen to be ethical can vary across:
  • individuals
  • groups
  • religions
  • cultures
    • Because of how wide digital culture has expanded globally, It is important that professionals at all levels determine what is ethically correct, What ethical eduication or training must be provided and how to promote ethical awareness in their organisation.
    Morals
    These are the personal beliefs about what is right and wrong which are usually shaped by cultiral, religious and societal influences.


    Acceptable Use Policy (AUP)

    An Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) outlines the rules and guidelines for using technology and information systems within an organization. It aims to ensure that all users understand their responsibilities and the consequences of violating these rules.

    This usually requires individuals to agree not to:
  • Use the service for any illegal activities
  • Hack into the security of any computer network or individual system
  • send spam or junk email to anyone who does not wish to recieve it
  • implement a DDoS attack by flooding the servers.


    • Autonomous Operation

    Autonomous systems are machines and software that can operate independently of human intervention (e.g., self driving cars).
    Across a range of sectors, technology can carry out tasks and processes on behalf of people, sometimes without the need of any human intervention. This allows the people them to focus on other tasks, improving overall productivity.

    What Can Go Wrong?

    Erroneous Data Can cause a system to be biased which may provide innacurate responses or unwanted outcomes, which in some casses can result in loss of revenue, customers and in the worst case scenario; The risk to life.

    To avoid this, The data used to develop the system should be monitored to ensure that there is no data that might negatively affect the functionality of the systemin any way.
    In some cases, human intervension may be required in the event of a malfunction or a third party has gained access to it's data; this is done for the system to be disabled, fixed or replaced to ensure security for the organisation, it's clients and employees.


    Changes in societal norms and behaviours and individuals

    Societal norms are unwritten rules about beliefs, attitudes and behaviours that are considered acceptable in a specific social group or culture.

    While digital technology has assisted in bridging the global gap, it has also created issues in some cases. for example, some people might have grown accustomed to communicating with people over the internet but at the same time, they cannot comunicate with those around them.
